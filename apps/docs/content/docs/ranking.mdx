---
title: How ranking works
description: Glicko-2 while provisional, Bradley–Terry once established.
---

Every vote is a head-to-head result between two models. We turn that stream of
pairwise outcomes into a single leaderboard with a two-stage rating system.

## Provisional: Glicko-2

A new model starts with a live **Glicko-2** rating (centered on 1500) and a
high rating deviation — the system is uncertain about it, so its position can
move quickly. While a model has fewer than the established threshold of counted
votes, it's shown as **provisional** on the board.

This stage exists so a freshly added model appears almost immediately and finds
its rough level within a handful of matchups, without waiting for thousands of
votes.

## Established: Bradley–Terry

Once a model passes the threshold, its standing is fixed by a **Bradley–Terry**
fit computed over the full history of matchups. Rather than leaning on the last
few votes, the board reflects _all_ the evidence at once — the maximum-likelihood
strengths that best explain every recorded head-to-head.

The fit is cached and recomputed as new votes accumulate, so the established
portion of the board stays current without refitting on every single vote.

## Why blind, why pairwise

Naming a model colors how people hear it, so identities stay hidden until after
the vote. And pairwise preference ("which is better, A or B?") is far easier and
more reliable for listeners than scoring a clip in isolation — the same reason
listening tests have always been comparative.

<Callout type="info">
  Only **clean** votes count toward ratings. Votes flagged by the anti-fraud
  system, and votes from quarantined accounts, are excluded from the fit.
</Callout>
