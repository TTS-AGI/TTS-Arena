---
title: How ranking works
description: One Bradley–Terry fit over every matchup, ranked by a conservative lower bound.
---

Every vote is a head-to-head result between two models. We turn that stream of
pairwise outcomes into a single leaderboard.

## One model: Bradley–Terry

The board is ranked by a single **Bradley–Terry** fit over the full history of
matchups. Bradley–Terry finds the maximum-likelihood "strength" of every model
that best explains every recorded head-to-head at once — so the ranking reflects
_all_ the evidence, not just the last few votes, and it's order-independent
(replaying the same votes always gives the same result).

Ratings are centered on ~1500. The fit is cached and recomputed as new votes
accumulate, so the board stays current without refitting on every single vote.

## Ranked by the lower bound, not the point estimate

We **display** each model's point rating, but we **rank** by a conservative
_lower bound_ — the bottom of its confidence interval. A model can't top the
board on a lucky handful of votes: it has to be both good **and** certain. As a
model plays more, its interval tightens and the displayed `± uncertainty`
shrinks. You'll see large error bars on new models and small ones on
well-established ones.

## Small samples are regularized

A maximum-likelihood fit has a sharp edge: a model that has only ever won (or
only ever lost) has an undefined "infinite" strength, which on a tiny sample
would produce a nonsense rating. We add a light **Bayesian prior** — a couple of
virtual, evenly-split games against an average opponent — to every model. This
pulls extreme records on tiny samples toward the center, and the effect
_vanishes_ as real games accumulate: by a few dozen votes it's negligible, and
by a few hundred it's gone entirely. It only ever disciplines the smallest
samples.

## When models appear

- A model must reach **100 counted votes** to appear on the default board —
  below that, the rating is too noisy to be meaningful.
- Below **300 votes** a model is badged **Preliminary**: it's ranked normally,
  but its rating is still settling and may move as more votes land.
- Newly added models with only a few votes are hidden by default; tick **“Show
  new models with few votes”** on the leaderboard to reveal them (with wide
  uncertainty bars).

## Why blind, why pairwise

Naming a model colors how people hear it, so identities stay hidden until after
the vote. And pairwise preference ("which is better, A or B?") is far easier and
more reliable for listeners than scoring a clip in isolation — the same reason
listening tests have always been comparative.

<Callout type="info">
  Only **clean** votes count toward ratings. Votes flagged by the anti-fraud
  system, and votes from quarantined accounts, are excluded from the fit.
</Callout>
